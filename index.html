The Story So Far

Do not follow where the path may lead. Go instead where there is no path and leave a trail.

- Ralph Waldo Emerson

When I was young, the thought of pursuing Electrical Engineering seemed daunting. I was uncertain if I had the aptitude to excel in a field that combined intricate mathematics, complex circuit designs, and rapidly evolving technology. My initial hesitation stemmed from self-doubt and fear of the unknown. But life has a way of steering us towards our true calling, and for me, that calling became evident as I delved deeper into the world of electronics, systems, and innovation.

During my undergraduate studies in Electrical and Electronics Engineering, I faced significant academic challenges, struggling to maintain strong scores. However, I discovered a passion for hands-on learning in the laboratories, where I thrived. Labs like the Electronics Laboratory and Object-Oriented Programming Laboratory allowed me to explore concepts through practical application, fueling my curiosity and providing a much-needed sense of clarity. My real turning point came when I stepped out of my comfort zone to lead a team for the ARM Design Challenge 2016. We designed a Smart Helmet that could alert nearby ambulances and family members in the event of an accident. Despite battling severe power outages caused by the Vardah cyclone, I successfully developed the prototype, overcoming both technical and personal hurdles. This experience was pivotal—it taught me the value of perseverance and demonstrated that fear and doubt could be conquered through determination and hard work.

After completing my undergraduate degree, I recognized the gaps in my foundational knowledge and took proactive steps to address them. Using resources like Khan Academy, I strengthened my understanding of mathematics and science while simultaneously exploring emerging fields like machine learning and renewable energy. This self-driven effort laid the foundation for my professional journey, which began as a Video Data Analyst at Valeo, where I contributed to self-driving car projects. Later, as a Machine Learning Data Associate at Amazon, I honed my skills in data analysis, consistently surpassing quality metrics and mentoring new team members. These experiences reinforced my belief in the importance of resilience, adaptability, and continuous learning.

Despite my early struggles in academics, I chose not to let my past define my future. When I embarked on my Master’s journey in Electrical Engineering at the University at Buffalo, I was determined to approach challenges with a renewed sense of purpose and discipline. This mindset has been transformative—today, my GPA stands at 3.8, a testament to the progress I’ve made and the lessons I’ve learned along the way. Courses like Power Electronics, Industrial Control Systems, and Photovoltaic Technology have allowed me to develop a deep understanding of the field while working on impactful projects, including the design and simulation of electric vehicle traction systems and the development of IoT-based safety devices.

If there’s one thing my journey has taught me, it’s that failure is not the end but the beginning of countless opportunities. Each setback has been a stepping stone toward greater growth, teaching me resilience, adaptability, and the importance of learning from mistakes. Failure has shown me that true progress lies in perseverance and the courage to try again. By embracing failure as a natural part of the learning process, I’ve unlocked opportunities I never imagined possible, transforming challenges into triumphs and doubts into achievements. I carry this philosophy with me as I continue to explore and contribute to the world of Electrical Engineering.
